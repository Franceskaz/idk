Siberian Husky

The Siberian Husky is a stunning, clever dog with boundless activity that has long been linked to chilly, severe winter climes and the sport of dog sledding. Even though they might be difficult to train, the breed is renowned for being delightfully friendly despite their "wolfish" appearance.

Shiba Inu

The Shiba Inu dog breed was developed in Japan about 2,300 years ago for hunting small animals and ground birds. It weighs 17 to 23 pounds and is a strong dog that stands 13.5 to 16.5 inches (34 to 42 cm) tall at the shoulders (7.5 to 10.5 kg). Shiba Inus are renowned for their self-assurance, perkiness, and triangularly set eyes.

German Shepard

German Shepherds are a large-sized working dog breed that were trained to be obedient, loyal, and clever, making them ideal for service work. They first appeared in Germany in the late 1800s. This comprises police academy, search and rescue, guarding, and herding.
